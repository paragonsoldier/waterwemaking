# Welcome 2024! | Resolutions for the New Year
## Introduction

_resolution: a firm decision to do or not to do something_

Each year I've written down my resolutions and had those firm decisions limply go uncompleted only to be set again in next year's notebook. I need a new strategy in 2024 if I'm to be successful. This year I'll focus on using 4 keys for setting my goals in order to finally achieve them. Goals need to be: measurable, attainable, accounted for, and ordered. 

## Keys to Setting Goals

_measurable: something that can be quantified_
Losing weight is a common New Year's resolution. However, if the losing weight is the full definition of the goal, then how much? 

_attainable: capable of being accomplished_
Weight lose goals can be made measurable by setting an amount to lose, but what amount should be set to keep the goal attainable? Too high of a bar may not be attainable. Through diet and exercise a person will eventually start building muscle. Muscle weighs more than fat. A scale may show a person is gaining weight, which would be contrary to their goal, but if the weight gain is muscle, their health is improving. In this example, a better, more attainable goal may be to decrease body fat percentage. 

_How do you eat an elephant? One bite at a time._
The key to achieving larger goals or projects is breaking the task down into smaller pieces. An over-arching goal may seem overwhelming, but shifting focus to smaller measurable and achievable tasks that lead to end goal will build confidence, consistency, and momentum. 

This is how I choose to set my health goal this year. Rather than focus on achieving a certain weight and getting discouraged, I choose to set a push-up goal of 1,825 pushups. This equates to 5 push-ups a day. By changing my focus to a smaller goal I'm granting myself flexibility in achieving it. If I miss a day, then I just do 10 push-ups the next day. As I grow stronger, I can choose to do more push-ups in a day to reach it faster. I can always push the goal further if I choose to, but by starting small I can prevent the discouragement that's caused my failure in past years.

_accountable: accepting responsibility for one's actions_
Does your actions move you closer to or further away from your goal? Eating a bag of Funyuns each day will cause me to gain, instead of lose, weight. Having a community, partner, friend question your actions and/or making your actions publicly visible will hold you accountable for them. By being held accountable you're likely to ensure your actions are positive. 

_order: the arrangement or disposition of people or things in relation to each other according to a particular sequence, pattern, or method._
I am a self-declared renaissance man. I have many hobbies and love to learn just about anything. It's easy for me to set too many tasks for myself, which leads to becoming overwhelmed and achieving few, if any, of them. It's impossible to do everything at once. This has been my biggest downfall over the last several years. Therefore, while I have many goals this year, I'm focusing on a subset of priorities first. This ensures I complete a goal before starting towards a new one and ensures I stop writing the same list each year.
